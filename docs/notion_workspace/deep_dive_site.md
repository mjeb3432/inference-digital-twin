## Why This Stage Matters

Site and permitting choices set hard boundaries for schedule, cost, and achievable power capacity. These decisions are upstream constraints, not downstream details.

## Key Decision Areas

- market selection and regulatory environment
- parcel size and expansion potential
- permitting track (standard, expedited, pre-permitted)
- utility expansion feasibility

## Typical Failure Modes

- target MW exceeds real site envelope
- permitting schedule ignored in go-live planning
- land cost assumptions not adjusted by market and permitting strategy

## Best-Practice Checklist

1. confirm zoning and utility interconnect feasibility early
2. model best/base/worst permit timeline bands
3. include land multiplier effects in capex planning
4. keep an explicit expansion assumption in scenario notes

## What to Compare Across Scenarios

- permit cost and time
- site max MW vs target MW gap
- land cost sensitivity by city/market

## Decision Output

A valid site decision should answer: can this location satisfy target capacity and schedule without relying on unverified expansion assumptions?
